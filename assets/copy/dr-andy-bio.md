# Meet Dr. Andy Burdette

*Jackson Veterinary Clinic — Bio for Web Designer*

## A Hometown Story

Jackson, Georgia isn't just where Dr. Andy Burdette practices veterinary medicine — it's where he grew up. His family moved to Jackson in 1981, the same year his father opened Jackson Veterinary Clinic. Andy attended school here from kindergarten through twelfth grade, building roots in a community that would later become the center of his professional life. Some of his clients today were once his teachers. Some were his classmates.

## A Life of Service

In June of 1997, Andy left Jackson to attend the United States Military Academy at West Point — one of the most demanding institutions in the country. West Point's mission is to build leaders of character committed to a lifetime of service to the Army and the Nation. Andy took that mission seriously. He served as an Army officer, deploying overseas in combat, and carried those values of duty, commitment, and service long after he hung up the uniform.

## Coming Home

After more than 20 years away, Dr. Burdette returned to Jackson in June of 2018. In November of 2023, he purchased the practice from his father — the same clinic that has served this community for over four decades. His parents still live in Jackson, in the same house they bought in 1981. Coming back wasn't just a career move. It was a commitment to the town that shaped him.

## Why Veterinary Medicine

When Dr. Burdette left the Army, he didn't leave behind the idea of service — he redirected it. He had grown up around veterinary medicine through his father and understood its importance. But what truly drew him in was something deeper: the bond between people and their pets. Research shows that 95% of pet owners consider their animals family members. That relationship — built on unconditional love, trust, and companionship — is one of the most meaningful connections in a person's life. Dr. Burdette wanted to protect it.

For him, caring for the pets of Jackson isn't just a profession. It's a way of continuing to serve the community he loves — by taking care of the family members who happen to have four legs.

## Jackson Veterinary Clinic Today

Under Dr. Burdette's leadership, Jackson Veterinary Clinic continues to grow while staying true to the community roots it was built on. Located at 369 East 2nd Street in Jackson, Georgia, the clinic offers comprehensive veterinary care for the pets of Butts County and the surrounding area. Dr. Burdette owns both the practice and the property — a long-term investment in the community's future.
