# Meet Dr. Alan Burdette

## Where It All Started

Dr. Alan Burdette grew up in Macon, Georgia and went on to earn his veterinary degree from the University of Georgia College of Veterinary Medicine in 1978. In 1981, he made a decision that would define the next four-plus decades of his life: he moved his young family to Jackson, Georgia and purchased what would become Jackson Veterinary Clinic. That practice has been serving Butts County ever since.

## Four Decades of Service to Jackson

For more than 40 years, Dr. Alan Burdette was the face of veterinary medicine in Jackson. He built the clinic from the ground up, earned the trust of generations of families in Butts County, and established a standard of care that the community came to rely on. In November of 2023, he passed the practice to his son, Dr. Andy Burdette — keeping it in family hands and ensuring that legacy would continue.

## Still in the Clinic

Retirement isn't really in Dr. Alan's vocabulary. He continues to work at Jackson Veterinary Clinic Tuesday through Thursday each week — performing surgeries on Tuesdays and Thursdays, and seeing patients on Wednesdays. His depth of experience and steady hands in the operating room are an invaluable part of what makes this clinic exceptional. Clients who have known him for years will be glad to know he's not going anywhere.

## Family

Dr. Alan and his wife Judy still live in Jackson in the same home they've had since 1981. Judy is a pharmacist at Piedmont Henry Hospital in Stockbridge. Together they have four children — Andy, Erin, Lindsay, and Mallory — and five grandchildren. Two of the four kids followed their dad into veterinary medicine: Dr. Andy Burdette now owns and runs Jackson Veterinary Clinic and Dr. Erin Burdette practices small animal medicine in California. Not a bad professional legacy.

## Outside the Clinic

When he's not in the clinic or working through Judy's to-do list, you'll likely find Dr. Alan on the golf course or at the lake. He's earned it.
